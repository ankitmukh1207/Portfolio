Research

Adaptive Quantum-Classical Learning for Multi-Crop Plant Disease Detection Using Variational Entanglement Transfer (AQCL-VET)

A novel hybrid quantum-classical architecture integrating parameterized quantum circuits (PQCs) with classical CNN feature extractors for multi-crop plant disease detection. The core innovation — Variational Entanglement Transfer (VET) — enables disease feature representations to transfer adaptively across crop domains, reducing labeled data requirements by 63%. Achieved 97.4% accuracy on MCPD-120K, outperforming ViT-B/16, EfficientNet-B4, and ResNet-50.

KIIT · School of Computer Engineering · Co-authored with Aryaman Sanskrityayan · Guide: Anjan Bandyopadhyay

Github link

IBM Quantum Eagle (127-Qubit) Validation — Quantum Kernel vs Classical RBF

Validated amplitude encoding and PQC feature extraction on IBM Quantum's ibm_sherbrooke 127-qubit Eagle processor. Hardware quantum kernel alignment achieved F1 of 0.887 vs 0.891 on noiseless simulator — only a 0.4% gap — and outperformed classical RBF kernels by 6.7% F1 on the cotton disease subtask, confirming quantum advantage in high-dimensional texture classification.

IBM Quantum Network · NSM India · DST Grant CRG/2024/004521
